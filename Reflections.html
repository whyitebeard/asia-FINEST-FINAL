SOCIT FEST

It was a long journey – started from scratch without a single guidance of previous officers it was hard but, we made it. At the pinnacle of each event I, as a host, organizer, officer, of three different IT organization, was happy to see their smile. Mentally and physically exhausted with less than 5 hours of sleep for three consecutive days just to make script, props, attend meetings, and balance workload as a student.

It seems that my personality as a “lazy” individual was removed – instantly, since at the beginning of my college days I knew that I would not be joining extracurricular activities, yet, here I am.


Honestly, I did not expect myself getting through these experiences because I have set my mind to focus on my studies on/off school and do my daily activities as an individual but, organization kick-in the door and shouted “You belong here”.

There were different hardships that I had encountered within this week, especially when I was appointed as a host at the same day and time for Ms. Rhea’s event given about less than 30 minutes to prepare before the said seminar.


It is indeed hard for me since I usually prepare 1 or 2 day/s before the event happens but, as they say, with great responsibility comes with great reliability – and so I stood up on the floor made some mistakes because of nervousness, luckily I kept my presence of my mind and apply all the necessary lessons that I’ve learned in the past and continue my role as a host.

After SoCIT Fest, I knew unto myself that, studying while having fun is not a bad idea at all. It makes you human compared when you do the same tasks every day and feel like a programmed robot. It doesn’t hurt to be a human, that is why students should know that college is not all about getting high grades, attending class, and wait ‘til class are suspended but, more than the school can offer is for us to be humans.
